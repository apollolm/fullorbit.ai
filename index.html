Frequently Asked Questions

The honest answers to questions every engineering leader asks about AI adoption.

Does this actually work, or is it just marketing hype?

Look, I get the skepticism. I felt the same way until I tried it myself. Earlier this year, I used Claude Code to build a complete feature that our team had estimated at 3 weeks. It took me 8 hours. That's when it clicked—this isn't about replacing good engineering practices, it's about amplifying them. From planning and design to implementation and testing, agentic AI can help accelerate the work that matters most.

Are you saying AI will replace our developers?

No. Your developers will still be developers—they'll just be much more productive. Think of it like giving them superpowers, not replacing them. Any work they're doing can be done faster, with better test coverage, more thorough review, and higher quality. They accomplish more, not different things.

What about security and compliance?

Fair question. Claude Code runs locally on your machine by default—it's not uploading your entire codebase to the cloud. For enterprise setups, we can configure zero data retention, VPC controls, or even air-gapped deployments. Both Anthropic and Google have the SOC 2 and compliance certifications you need.

What if my engineers don't trust AI-generated code?

Good. They shouldn't trust it blindly. AI is a powerful tool, not a replacement for thinking. But let's be honest about what Claude Code can actually do—it's like having a Senior Engineer, Analyst, QE, and technical writer all wrapped into one. The engineers I work with use it daily for key functionality development, troubleshooting, debugging, performance optimizations, planning, documentation, and iteration. You still review everything, you still make the important decisions, but now you have an incredibly capable collaborator who can handle complex work across the entire development lifecycle.

What happens if we don't adopt AI development tools?

We're at an inflection point. Teams that master agentic AI development will ship faster, solve harder problems, and take on challenges that would have been impossible before. Meanwhile, teams still doing everything manually will find themselves explaining why projects take months instead of weeks. The gap is widening fast—if you're one step behind today because you haven't started with agentic AI coding, you'll be five steps behind tomorrow. The longer you wait, the harder it becomes to catch up. It's not about replacing good engineering—it's about amplifying what your best people can accomplish.

Is this just technical training, or do you help with the organizational stuff too?

Both. The technical part is actually the easy part. The hard part is helping your leadership team figure out how to plan, resource, and manage when development suddenly gets much faster. I spend time with your leadership team to work through those challenges together.
